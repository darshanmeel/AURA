Most teams running AI coding agents have no idea where the spend goes. The invoice is one number; the work behind it is invisible.

One real month of Claude Code transcripts, one query: $2,666 — with a single agent quietly eating two-thirds of it. None of it visible the day before.

That's what Aura surfaces, and it's open for anyone to run. It's a local-first analytics platform for AI-coding-agent sessions: it reads the JSONL transcripts your agents already write, transforms them with dbt, and shows cost and attribution down to the individual prompt — including overkill detection: fix a typo on a top-tier model and it gets flagged. Everything runs in Docker on your own machine; nothing leaves your laptop. MIT-licensed.

Spend, with receipts.
