I ran one query against a month of my own Claude Code transcripts and found I'd spent $2,666 in 30 days — with a single agent quietly eating two-thirds of it. None of it was visible the day before.

So I built Aura and put it out in the open for anyone to use.

It's a local-first analytics platform for AI-coding-agent sessions. It reads the JSONL transcripts your agents already write, transforms them with dbt, and shows you cost and attribution down to the individual prompt — including overkill detection: fix a typo on a top-tier model and it gets flagged. Everything runs in Docker on your own machine; nothing leaves your laptop. MIT-licensed.

Spend, with receipts.
